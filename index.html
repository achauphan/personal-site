ABOUT PROJECTS

Hi, I'm Anderson - a first generation Asian-American student at the University of Minnesota and will be completing a B.A. in Computer Science along with a minor in IT Infrastructure this spring. I am passionate about building software & tools for the betterment of science, technology, and humanity as a whole.

Currently, I am working as a Student Research Software Engineer at the National Center for Atmospheric Research (NCAR) developing the open-sourced project DART. In addition to DART, I've started the development of a software testing interface for high-performance computing (HPC) platforms named Testmancer. Previous to NCAR, I worked as an IT help desk consultant at the Minnesota Supercomputing Institute (MSI) providing support & guidance to Univeristy researchers using HPC equipment.

As I graduate and complete my internship at NCAR, I hope to be able to continue contributing to the scientific research community by developing scientific software & tools to be used by researchers all around the world for the betterment of society. These are the values that I have learned to hold from the amazing mentors & people that I have worked with at NCAR, MSI, and researchers of the Univeristy of Minnesota.
